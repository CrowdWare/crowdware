Welcome to CrowdWare

Our goal is to design, create and deliver free software to the crowd, based on your problems or ideas.
We are also going to create hardware solutions which helps to make this planet a better place.
We are thinking about solar destillation to create drinking water and we will design solutions for tiny houses.

We want everybody to be able to live a selfsustained life in peace and freedom on this wonderful planet.

With CrowdWare, we aim to distribute software independently of existing structures and free of charge. Open source plays an important role here, as it allows the software to be protected from becoming patented. Patents hinder further development and impose licensing fees on end users that only benefit the patent holder.

Our goal with CrowdWare is to create a sustainable alternative in the tech landscape, prioritizing ethical practices and social responsibility. We want to foster an environment that encourages innovation without compromising our values or the well-being of society.

To put it positively, we want to bring something sustainable and good into the world, inspired by Joseph Beuys' concept of "social plastic," that grants us all peace and freedom.

In the event that we make profits, these profits will not be distributed to individuals within the company. Instead, they will be invested in projects that aim to reshape this world, making it a better place for all of us.

A major goal is to purchase land to protect it from speculation.
There, small villages with tiny houses, geodesic domes, yurts, and similar structures will be established, where people can live for free.
This idea originates from the UBUNTU movement in South Africa, where people live without money, without trade, and without commerce.
In Germany, we call it Exchange-Logic-Free.
Everyone in these communities does what they can, gives what they have, and receives what they need.
